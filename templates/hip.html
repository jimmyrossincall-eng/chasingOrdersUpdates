I am writing to document the medical necessity for a hip orthosis (HCPCS Code: {{ Selected_Brace }}) for my patient, {{ fname }} {{ lname }}, who is currently under my care for the treatment of {{ diagnosis }}.

Clinical Justification:

  • Recurrent hip instability and pain with weight-bearing activities
  • Significant weakness of hip abductors and flexors
  • Limited range of motion and difficulty with ambulation
  • Pain and discomfort affecting daily activities and sleep

Physical Exam:

  • Tenderness over greater trochanter and hip joint
  • Positive Trendelenburg sign indicating hip abductor weakness
  • Reduced internal and external rotation
  • Antalgic gait pattern observed

Imaging supports degenerative changes, joint space narrowing, and soft tissue involvement consistent with the diagnoses listed above.

Medical Necessity:

  • ☑ Stabilize the hip joint and provide compression support
  • ☑ Reduce pain and inflammation during ambulation
  • ☑ Improve mobility, gait mechanics, and functional capacity
  • ☑ Prevent further injury and support post-operative recovery
  • ☑ Facilitate participation in physical therapy

Conservative management including physical therapy, NSAIDs, and activity modification have provided only partial relief. The hip orthosis is essential to allow continued functional mobility and prevent further deterioration.
